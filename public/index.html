The Black Panther Party

The Black Panther Party was a revolutionary socialist organization that fought for racial justice, self-defense, and community empowerment.

About The Black Panther Party

Community Programs

The Black Panther Party was known for its community programs, such as free breakfast for children, health clinics, and educational initiatives, aimed at supporting the underserved communities.

Self-Defense and Empowerment

One of the core principles of the Black Panther Party was self-defense. They advocated for the protection of black communities from police brutality and racial oppression through organized community efforts.

Political Activism

The party also played a crucial role in advocating for political changes that addressed the racial inequality present in the United States. Their influence continues to resonate in modern activism.

Key Events in Black Panther Party History

1966 - Founding of the Party
1967 - Protest Against Police Brutality
1969 - Free Breakfast for Children Program
